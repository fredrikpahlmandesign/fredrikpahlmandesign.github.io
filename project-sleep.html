01 — The problem

One trial for everyone left value on the table

Sleep Cycle ran a single opt-out trial: a hard paywall up front, then a premium trial that quietly converted. It worked — but it asked for commitment before people had felt the value, and it gave us little room to learn.

We wanted a model that could flex to different users and let us experiment safely. So we started where the questions were — mapping the whole monetisation journey as an opportunity solution tree with the team.

02 — The strategy

Three models, one flexible spine

Rather than redesign a screen, we redesigned the shape of the offer — a spine that could run as opt-out today, freemium now, and reverse-trial next.

From wall to doorway

The reverse trial flips the ask: full Premium for 14 days, no card needed, then a graceful downgrade to free. People feel the value first, decide second.

03 — Onboarding & activation

Earn the trial by understanding the person

Before asking for anything, we ask what better sleep means to them — then frame Premium around that goal. Activation is the real conversion event, so the flow leads with personalisation, not price.

Each step is narrated below — goal-setting, the journey framing, and an honest, gentle data-permission prime.

Discovery → Experiment → Outcome

Users didn't know what Sleep Cycle did for them — so they weren't ready to pay for it.

In weekly discovery sessions we kept hearing the same thing: users hitting the paywall didn't understand what they'd get from a subscription. The onboarding had walked them through setup, but hadn't shown them the product in action. They arrived at the paywall curious but unpersuaded.

The hypothesis was simple: show them what the product does before asking them to pay. We added a short UI reel — a few seconds of the core experience — directly into the onboarding flow, right before the paywall. We ran it as an A/B test.

The feel

Premium should feel like a calmer place

Working with brand & motion, we gave the experience a dreamlike, nighttime quality — so the upgrade reads as an atmosphere, not a transaction.

04 — Paywall & trial lifecycle

One value story, told across every moment

The paywall isn't a single screen — it's a sequence that runs from first offer through the trial to the downgrade. Each touch repeats the same promise in the user's language.

Lead with the outcome

"Wake up feeling rested" before "299 kr/year." The hero image and headline sell the morning; the price is calm, singular, and reversible.

Then show the substance

A scannable feature list backs the promise for people who want detail — richer data, sleep sounds, programs — without burying the decision.

Even the downgrade is a moment

When the trial ends, "You're now on the free plan" reassures rather than punishes — core tracking stays, and the door back to Premium is always warm. That respect is what makes the next offer land.

05 — Outcome & reflection

A model that can keep learning

The biggest win wasn't a single number — it was turning monetisation into something we could test, in a system the whole team understood and a tone that fit the brand.

A 6.7% subscription lift from a single onboarding experiment validated the discovery process as much as it validated the design. The cadence — weekly sessions, fast experiments, real data — is what made it possible.
